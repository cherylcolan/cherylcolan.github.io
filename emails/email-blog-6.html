When your food is out to get you

View this email in your browser

What if I told you smoking cigarettes is better for you than eating fruits and vegetables? Would you laugh out loud and call me crazy? Maybe you would refute my argument and cite a long list of reasons to defend your position. Your reaction would most likely be tied to a deep-seated belief that smoking leads to chronic diseases and death. You would not be wrong, smoking is deadly. Now, what if I told you some of the same chemicals you are trying to avoid by not smoking are making it into your body via your dinner plate? Read on and discover the “dirty dozen” of the produce world that may be doing you more harm than good.

Cigarettes contain highly toxic and carcinogenic (cancer-causing) substances like arsenic, carbon monoxide and tar. Likewise, pesticides used in fruit and vegetable farming contain their own special batch of carcinogens, such as insecticides to kill insects, fungicides to kill fungus, and rodenticides to kill rodents.

The “dirty dozen” are the top 12 fruits and vegetables that carry the greatest risk of pesticide contamination and health hazards for the consumer. Making the yearly list are those with skins and rinds that offer little or no protection from the toxic substances used in their growing.  Learn more »

Listed from most contaminated to least

  1. Peaches
  2. Apples
  3. Sweet Bell peppers
  4. Celery
  5. Nectarines
  6. Strawberries
  7. Cherries
  8. Pears
  9. Imported grapes
  10. Spinach
  11. Lettuce
  12. Potatoes

Before you swear off these wonderful sources of vital nutrients and healthy snacks, consider the alternative. Organic produce offers us a way to enjoy our favorite fruits and vegetables in a safe and natural way. The added cost of buying organic is minimal compared to the expense of poor health. Be a savvy shopper and spend the extra on organics while trimming your budget of processed and manufactured foods. Your body will thank you! Look for our shopping guide for good health next week!
